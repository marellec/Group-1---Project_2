Issues and User-Testing Methods

We found that the CBord mobile app is not well designed, when you search for a door in a certain building on campus the app:

  1. Scrolls you down the list to the first matching result.
  2. This list isn't in alphabetical order, so you might have to scroll up or down more to find other doors in the same building.
  3. Moves your recent search to the top so that the next time you search for another door in that building, it always takes you to the top.

This provoked us to investigate the application more. After auditing the application for its usability, accessibility, and functionality, we found that the application had plenty of issues. Some of which we believe can be solved by updating the UI, while some might be back-end server related issues.

User Survey

Users responded to a survey detailing their experience using the app and how easy they found signing in, opening doors, and moving around through the app.

The results are outlined here: Survey Results

User Task

We had users perform these simple tasks in the app and observed their ability to complete them:

  1. Login using school credentials
  2. Open a door (any door)
  3. Open the E1 North Entrance without using search
  4. Using the search feature, open Kaplan West Main Door
  5. Without connecting to wifi, open door Crown Lower Level exterior East

We recorded observations of each user's actions, and interviewed them about their overall experience.
